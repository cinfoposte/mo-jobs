How can you make a difference?

UNICEF's Global Shared Services Centre in Budapest is recruiting highly dedicated individuals for technical and advisory support, ensuring high-quality service delivery, performance excellence, and full compliance in managing consultancy contracts across both pre- and post-contracting phases. Also manages a team of contract processing staff and involves active engagement with contracting managers in offices across the globe, offering expert guidance on procedures, compliance standards, and best practices.

Key functions, accountabilities, and related tasks:

Under the supervision of the Finance Manager provides day-to-day operational and advisory support, ensuring high-quality service delivery, performance excellence, and full compliance in managing consultancy contracts across both pre and post-contracting phases. The Contracts Officer oversees the end-to-end lifecycle of individual consultancy contracts - including reviewing Terms of Reference (TORs), supporting sourcing and selection, preparing offers, issuing contracts, and managing closure processes. In addition, the Contracts Officer manages all escalations from the offices and contracting managers.

Minimum requirements:

Education:

  • A completed university degree in Business Administration, Management, Human resources, Procurement, International Development, or related field is required.

Work Experience:

  • A minimum of two (2) years of relevant experience at the national and international level in contracting, procurement, administration, human resource or other directly related technical fields is required, preferably with the UN/UNICEF or another international organization is required.
  • Previous experience in planning, organizing and delivering consultancy administrative support services is highly desirable.
  • Work experience in team leadership in a shared services center context is an asset.
  • Knowledge of UN/UNICEF's rules, regulations and procedures is considered an asset.

Skills:

  • Ability to supervise and direct a team of transaction processing staff is highly desirable.
  • Good knowledge of latest developments and technology in individual consultancy management is highly desirable
  • In depth understanding of individual consultancy processes, from programme needs assessment, through planning, hiring, selection and contracting, and service delivery, monitoring and evaluation is desirable.
  • Good knowledge and understanding of UNICEF programmes, financial and legal aspects of contracting ethics and risk management of consultant contracting is an asset.
  • Good analytical and negotiating skills is an asset.
  • Ability to work in a multicultural environment and establish harmonious working relationships, both within and outside the organization is an asset
  • Fast learner, adapts, responds and owns change, tolerates ambiguity is an asset.
  • Ability to contribute to formulation of strategies and policies is an asset.

Language Requirements:

  • Fluency in both spoken and written English is required.
  • Working knowledge of French and/or Spanish is an advantage. Knowledge of another official UN language (Arabic, Chinese or Russian) is an asset.
